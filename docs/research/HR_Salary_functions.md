## HR Salary Management Functions

#### Compensation Strategy & Philosophy

This pillar defines how the organization thinks about pay at a business level.

* Compensation Strategy: HR defines how the company wants to pay people.
* External Competitiveness: HR checks whether company pay is competitive with the external job market.
* Market Benchmarking: HR checks what similar companies are paying for similar roles.
* Executive and Critical Role Compensation: HR manages specialized compensation structures for senior leadership and business-critical roles.

#### Job Structure & Pay Architecture

This pillar creates the internal structure needed to make compensation consistent and scalable.

* Job Architecture: HR defines the structure of jobs inside the company.
* Salary Bands / Pay Ranges: HR creates salary ranges for each role and level.
* Location and Currency Management: HR handles compensation differences across countries, cities, currencies, and local pay practices.

#### Salary Decisions & Employee Lifecycle

This pillar covers salary decisions made during an employee’s journey in the company.

* New Hire Compensation: HR manages salary decisions for new joiners.
* Salary Revisions / Increments: HR manages periodic salary changes.
* Promotion Compensation: HR decides how salary changes when employees move to higher roles.
* Employee Lifecycle Salary Events: HR manages salary changes across joining, confirmation, transfer, promotion, retention, and exit.
* Salary Exceptions: HR reviews and governs compensation cases that fall outside standard policy or salary bands.

#### Rewards, Benefits & Total Compensation

This pillar covers everything beyond base salary and looks at the full value of employee compensation.

* Variable Pay / Bonus Management: HR manages bonuses, incentives, commissions, and other performance-linked payouts.
* Benefits and Allowances: HR manages non-base-pay compensation such as insurance, allowances, retirement benefits, and perks.
* Total Compensation / CTC Management: HR tracks the full cost and value of an employee’s compensation package.
* Performance-Linked Pay: HR connects salary increments, bonuses, and rewards with employee performance.

#### Fairness, Retention & Workforce Health

This pillar ensures compensation is fair, motivating, and aligned with retention goals.

* Internal Pay Equity: HR ensures employees doing similar work at similar levels are paid fairly.
* Retention and Attrition Risk: HR identifies employees whose compensation may create resignation or retention risk.
* Manager Guidance: HR helps managers make fair, consistent, and budget-aware salary decisions.
* Employee Communication: HR communicates salary changes, offer details, increments, bonuses, and compensation policies clearly to employees.

#### Budgeting, Planning & Business Control

This pillar connects salary management with finance, planning, and company affordability.

* Compensation Budgeting: HR works with Finance to plan salary budgets.
* Compensation Planning Cycles: HR manages structured annual or periodic salary review processes.
* Workforce Cost Forecasting: HR estimates future salary costs based on hiring plans, increments, attrition, and promotions.
* Payroll Coordination: HR ensures payroll teams receive accurate and approved salary inputs.

#### Governance, Compliance & Analytics

This pillar ensures compensation decisions are controlled, legal, auditable, and data-driven.

* Compliance and Legal Requirements: HR ensures salary practices follow country-specific labor, tax, and pay regulations.
* Compensation Policy Management: HR creates and maintains policies for salary reviews, bonuses, promotions, allowances, and exceptions.
* Compensation Governance: HR defines who can view, recommend, approve, change, and export salary-related information.
* Audit and Change Tracking: HR maintains a record of salary decisions, approvals, changes, reasons, and effective dates.
* Data Privacy and Access Control: HR ensures sensitive salary data is visible only to authorized users.
* Reporting and Analytics: HR tracks compensation trends, salary distribution, budget usage, pay gaps, and workforce cost.
